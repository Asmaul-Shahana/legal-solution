The Legal Practice Area

There are several types of law you'll come across during your legal studies. On this page, you'll find a comprehensive list of different areas of law you can choose to specialise in.

Business Law

Business law is a crucial component of law in general since, without it, the corporate, manufacturing, and retail sectors would all be under a dictators rule.

Criminal Law

Criminal law is only one of the devices by which organized societies protect the security of individual interests and ensure the survival of the group.

Child Support

Our team is here to provide an accurate assessment of your childs needs and rights prior to getting child support. Our team is prepared to help you with your family law matters.

Education Law

Education law is a set of rules and regulations that guide the workings of an education system. Many regions have detailed educational law systems.

Divorce Law

Divorce is the process of terminating a marriage or marital union. It is also known as dissolution of marriage. Divorce allows each former partner to marry another person.

Tax Law

Tax law, body of rules under which a public authority has a claim on taxpayers, requiring them to transfer to the authority part of their income or property.

Flexbox provides a one dimensional layout. Where as Grid provides multi-dimensional layout.

Flexbox is not easily managable but Grid is easy to manipulate

Flexbox focuses on content flow and Grid focuses on content placement/layout first.

Media Query is used to make the website responsive. Media queries allow us to apply CSS styles depending on a device's general type, to create different layouts depending on the size of the viewport. There are some standard breakpoints. We can design our website as mobile-first or desktop-first way depending on the requirements. CSS frameworks like Bootstrap, Tailwind uses mobile-first approach.

The CSS box model is essentially a box that wraps around every HTML element. It consists of: margins, borders, padding, and the actual content.

  • The content area, bounded by the content edge, contains the "real" content of the element, such as text, an image, or a video player.
  • The padding area, bounded by the padding edge, extends the content area to include the element's padding.
  • The border area, bounded by the border edge, extends the padding area to include the element's borders.
  • The margin area, bounded by the margin edge, extends the border area to include an empty area used to separate the element from its neighbors.
